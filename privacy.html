Hello, and welcome to our Privacy Policy. This Privacy Policy helps you to make informed decisions about your relationship with us, so please read it carefully. We hope you're sitting comfortably in a boring lecture. Here we go...

1 Introduction

The Privacy Policy is here to do three things:

  1. Explain the way we use information that you share with us in order to build a great product and give you a great experience with it;
  2. Ensure that you understand what information we collect with your permission, and what we do -- and do not do -- with it;
  3. Hold us accountable for protecting your rights and your privacy under this policy.

All the information we collect is related to providing MCU App and its features.

Broadly speaking, there are two categories of information we collect: 1) information that we must have in order for you to use MCU App; and 2) information that we can use to provide additional features and improve our application accordingly; and 3) information that helps us conduct studies on Medical Education.

The first category includes:

  • Registration information, which includes your student ID only. Your MCU password, for example, will remain only on your device.
  • The lessons you are required to attend and related information. This allows us to complete our extensive database on tutors, lectures, and student satisfaction with them.
  • Technical and sensor information necessary to operate MCU App. This includes the type of device you use, data from the touchscreen, and information from your device's accelerometer and gyroscope sensors. This enables you to control MCU App and allows us to do simple things like providing timely alerts and/or determining the best time to ask for your opinion.

The second category includes information that enables us to offer you additional features. We automatically enable sharing of such information upon install, but you are able to opt out while keep on using the app. Here are some examples:

  • Your specific location: This information enables us to create collaborative learning experiences (only with others who have also given permission).
  • Your app usage pattern: This includes information on frequently accessed pages, duration of app usage, settings, and frequency of using each function provided by the App.

The distinction between these two categories is important. Information in the first category is information you must provide in order to use MCU App. When you agree to our Privacy Policy, you give us the right to collect this information and use it for the purposes described. Information in the second category is information we will stop collecting if you explicitly choose to.

For the third category, we are collecting information to further improve medical education in CUHK via different quantitative and qualitative case studies. We will prompt you regularly for comments and ratings on specific lectures, tutorials, or teachers. We will collect these information on a anonymous basis with all identifiable personal information obfuscated before publication.

We will be clear with you about how and when we might share information.

We hope this helps you to understand our privacy commitments to you. Now, the details await you below, but please remember that this Introduction is part of the Privacy Policy itself -- it's our promise to you. If you ever have any questions or concerns, then please let us know at mkcyyin@gmail.com.

Throughout this Privacy Policy we refer to the MCU App or the App.

5 Sharing information

This section describes how the information collected or generated through your use of the SMU service may be shared by you or by us.

5.1 Sharing by you

5.1.1 Generally

The MCU App is a learning application that offers many ways to view and share content. We encourage you to take advantage of these social features with others on the App.

Your name and/or student ID, will not be publicly available. All information you submit will be displayed anonymously in the system.

What you post, comment, or upload to the App will remain publicly available. Please consider removing identifiable personal information before posting, commenting or uploading.

Learn more about how to manage notifications and what you share with others in Your preferences below.

5.2 Sharing by us

5.2.1 Research

We may share information with research partners in order to conduct educational research. The information we share is in a de-identified format (for example, through the use of hashing) that does not personally identify you.
